Privacy Policy

Timer Police

👋 Hi there! We wrote this privacy policy to be honest and easy to understand — no confusing legal language, no hidden surprises. Timer Police is built around trust, and this page reflects that. If you ever have a question, just email us.
🌿

So, what is Timer Police?

Timer Police is a digital wellbeing app for Android that helps you be more intentional about how you use your phone. You choose which apps to limit, set a time budget, and Timer Police gently steps in when that time is up — giving you a moment to pause and decide.

It's not about taking control away from you. It's about giving it back.

📋

What information does the app use?

To do its job, Timer Police works with a small set of information:

  • The list of installed apps (app names/package names) so you can choose what to protect
  • The app names you personally select to control — nothing else
  • Session records — simple timestamps of when a session started and how it ended (kept promise, extended, or ignored)
  • Foreground app activity — only for the apps you've selected, so we know when to show a timer
  • Purchase metadata - a purchase token and product ID when you buy Pro, processed locally
We never peek at apps you haven't chosen. Your other app usage is completely invisible to us.
🎯

Why do we use this information?

Every piece of data we use has one clear purpose — making the app work for you:

  • The installed apps list lets you pick what to protect
  • App names tell us which apps to watch for you
  • Session records help you see your own progress over time
  • Foreground activity lets us show your timer at the right moment
  • Local purchase checks let us unlock Pro for lifetime access

That's it. We don't analyse trends, build profiles, or use your data to show you ads.

📡

What leaves your device?

Almost everything stays right on your phone — your selected apps, session records, and usage history are all stored locally on your device and never leave it.

The installed apps list is processed locally on your device and is not shared.

The only data that may leave your device is handled by Google Play when you buy Pro. Timer Police does not run its own servers for purchases. Payment details are handled entirely by Google Play.

We never transmit your accessibility data off your device. It is used locally only, to display timers and prompts.
♿

About the Accessibility Service

Timer Police uses Android's Accessibility Service for one specific reason — to detect when you open an app you've chosen to manage, so it can show your timer or a gentle interruption screen.

This is the only way Android allows an app to do this reliably. We use it carefully and responsibly.

Timer Police does not use Accessibility Service data for any purpose unrelated to the core app functionality described above. It is never used for advertising, tracking, profiling, or shared with any third party.
🔗

Third-party services we rely on

We use a small number of trusted services to keep Timer Police running smoothly:

  • Google Play Billing — for one-time purchases

These are reputable, industry-standard services. Your payment details go directly through Google Play — Timer Police never sees, touches, or stores your payment information at any point.

🤝

Do you sell or share our data?

No — and we want to be very direct about this:

  • We do not sell your personal data to anyone, ever
  • We do not share your accessibility data with any third party
  • We do not share your installed apps list with anyone
  • We only share data with the services that are essential to run the app (like Google Play Billing)
Your data belongs to you. We are simply trusted to look after it while you use the app.
🗂️

How long do we keep your data?

We keep your data only as long as it's useful to you:

  • All app-control settings and session records are stored on your device and disappear when you clear the app or uninstall it
  • Pro access status is stored locally and can be restored through Google Play
  • Pro purchases are tied to the Google Play account used at purchase time. Reinstalling from Google Play on the same account should restore access. If you switch accounts or install outside Google Play (for example, ADB/sideload), restoration may not work.
🎛️

You are always in control

We want you to feel completely in charge of your own experience:

  • You can disable the Accessibility Service at any time in your Android Settings
  • You can uninstall the app at any time — no penalties, no retention
  • You can manage purchases through Google Play
👶

Children's privacy

Timer Police does not request or collect personal information. The app runs locally and only uses device data needed for its core features. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided personal information, contact us and we will delete it.

🛡️

How we protect your data

Timer Police keeps your data on your device and relies on Android's built-in protections (such as app sandboxing and permission controls) to keep it safe.

🔄

Updates to this policy

We may occasionally update this privacy policy as the app grows or as rules change. When we do, we'll update the date at the bottom of this page. If the changes are significant, we'll let you know through the app.

Continuing to use Timer Police after an update means you're comfortable with the changes. If you're ever unsure, feel free to reach out and ask us directly.
